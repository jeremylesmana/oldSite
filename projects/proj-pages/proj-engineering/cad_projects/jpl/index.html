JPL Invention Challenge

          The JPL invention challenge is a competition held annually hosted by NASA. The competition is designed for teams of students to compete against other teams in local schools. Every year students are instructed to design a mechanism to perform a certain task, and the team with the most efficient and effective way to perform that task with their mechanism wins. There will be a referee on the spot to start and stop a stopwatch and judge our results. There will also be other judges and staff members inspecting and judging our mechanisms.

School Strategy

          For our school, we will hold an intramural competition amongst the different Advanced CAD classes. The top 3 teams with the fastest time will be qualified to proceed to the JPL competition and have a chance to win first place in the competition.

Mission

          Our mission this year for the JPL invention challenge is to design a mechanism that can transport 250 grams of water from a plastic cup to a water vessel 2 meters away. The team with the fastest time and least spillage wins the competition. There will also be a plastic fish placed in the first cup that has to be safely transferred as well. The mechanism must be switched by a one-time trigger such as buttons, levers, cutting a string, etc... and from then on the machine must perform its actions by itself.
